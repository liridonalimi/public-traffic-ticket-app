# Module 18 - Albanian-First Localization

## Outcome

Module 18 provides complete Albanian and English interfaces through Android locale resources. On first use, the app follows the device language when supported and falls back to English for unsupported device languages. A persistent in-app selector lets the operator choose `Use device language`, `Shqip`, or `English` without depending on the tablet's system-language list.

The driver, passenger, operations, reporting, route, fare, printing, GPS, stop-request, and synchronization experiences change language without changing ticket or synchronization contracts.

## Kosovo operator terminology

- `Shoferi`, `Autobusi`, `Linja`, `Ndalesa`, and `Turni` replace generic English workflow labels.
- Cash sales use `para të gatshme` and ticket prices remain in euro.
- Demo fares are `E rregullt`, `Student`, `Pensionist 65+`, and `Fëmijë`.
- Demo routes and stops use Albanian names, including `Qendër`, `Stacioni Qendror`, `Bulevardi Nënë Tereza`, and `Bregu i Diellit`.
- Albanian characters such as `ë` and `ç` are stored directly as UTF-8 Android resources.

## Language behavior

The base `values/strings.xml` catalog is Albanian and `values-en/strings.xml` provides English. The application locale manager resolves system-default behavior explicitly: Albanian device locales use Albanian, English device locales use English, and unsupported device locales use English. An explicit app-language choice overrides the device and is saved locally.

Dynamic counts, identifiers, cash amounts, stop names, and report values use formatted or plural resources. Runtime messages generated by the view model and device integrations also use the active locale.

## Tablet validation checklist

1. Leave the selector on `Use device language` and confirm it matches the supported device language or uses English when the device language is unsupported.
2. Open `Operations & Setup`, select `Shqip`, and confirm the app recreates in Albanian.
   Confirm the dropdown closes cleanly and the app remains in Operations and Setup.
3. Complete sign-in, bus and route selection, and shift start.
4. Check `ë` and `ç`, long button labels, cards, and dialogs for clipping.
5. Sell and print each fare type; review the passenger display and end-shift dialog.
6. Synchronize a closed shift and open Operations and the administrative report.
7. Select `English` inside the app and repeat the main driver workflow.
8. Confirm the explicit choice persists after restarting the app and saved shifts and tickets remain available.
9. Return the selector to `Use device language`.

## Acceptance criteria

- System default follows supported device languages, with English fallback for unsupported languages.
- Albanian and English can be selected and persisted inside the app.
- The complete Android workflow has no embedded English-only UI labels.
- Kosovo-oriented route, fare, shift, and cash terminology is consistent.
- Language changes do not alter stored identifiers, ticket values, or synchronization payloads.
- Existing Module 17 role separation and shift-ending safeguards remain intact.

## Validation status

Complete. Automated tests, debug assembly, and Android lint passed. The Albanian and English workflows, saved language selection, locale switching, dropdown transition, driver flow, passenger display, reporting, printing, shift closure, and synchronization were validated on the pilot device.
